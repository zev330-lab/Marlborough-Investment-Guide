False Positive Audit – Opportunity Set

This audit reviews each property in the Opportunity Set to identify potential false positives—deals that appear attractive only because of optimistic or incomplete inputs—and to separate higher‑confidence opportunities from those requiring further verification. The key issues examined include unknown HOA fees, insurance costs, repair liabilities, regulatory hurdles (ADUs, condo bylaws), tenant‑law implications and liquidity concerns. Properties are grouped by strategy/tab and split into Recommended (highest confidence) and Watchlist (needs verification or high risk).

Conclusion

Only 35 High St emerges as a high‑confidence opportunity under current assumptions. The remaining properties depend on optimistic rent projections, low expense assumptions or uncertain regulatory approvals. These watchlist deals require thorough due diligence—obtaining HOA budgets, inspecting building systems, verifying zoning and ADU rules, and incorporating realistic insurance and repair reserves—before proceeding.
